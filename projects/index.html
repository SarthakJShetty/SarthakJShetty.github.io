Probabilistic Exploration Strategies for Heterogenous Robot Swarms
90/100
  • Developing coordination & exploration strategies for multi-UAV swarm to conduct reconnaissance of survivors in flooded areas. Advised by Professor Debasish Ghose at the Mobile Robotics Laboratory, Department of Aerospace Engineering, Indian Institute of Science.
  • Exploration strategy based on novel probabilistic “weight-based” algorithm.
  • Currently deploying the probabilistic algorithm for multi-UAV system comprising of 3DR Iris Quadcopters on ROS & Gazebo, using MAVROS/MAVLINK as a communication protocol.
Biases in academic publications
60/100
  • A significant proportion of scientific publications pertaining to peninsular India in the late 90's have been motivated by economic incentives. We are building a natural-language understanding toolkit to prove the same, by scraping data from different scientific repositories.
  • Working in collaboration with Vijay Ramesh from the Department of Ecology, Evolution and Environmental Biology, Columbia University, and Anand MO from the Nature Conservation Foundation.
  • Abstracts are scraped and keywords are identified in different publications across scientific repositories. The overlap between different keywords are hypothesized to account for such biases and trends in research.

Completed Projects

Transiently Informed Robotic Swarms
  • Part of undergraduate thesis research on developing coordination & exploration strategies for robotic swarms involved in search & rescue, inspired by traneintly informed ant swarms. Advised by Professor Vishwesha Guttal and Professor Debasish Ghose.
  • Model designed on ROS using Frontier Exploration, Adaptive Monte Carlo Localization and move_base.
  • Exploration stack is being prototyped on a swarm of Clearpath Huskies, each mounter with a LiDAR to map the environment around it in three dimensions.
Biomimetic Robotic Fish
  • Built a predatory robotic fish, as a part of Summer Research Internship at the Theoretical Ecology & Evolution Laboratory
  • The Robotic Fish will be used to research collective behavior and aggregation patterns in Serpae tetra species of fish.
  • Worked under the guidance of Professor Vishwesha Guttal from Center for Ecological Sciences, Indian Institute of Science.
Differential Drive Donkey Car
  • Built a small-scale differential drive autonomous car, powered by a TensorFlow based Autopilot, trained on nearly 10,000 images of a track.
  • The Autopilot was used to drive the vehicle autonomously around the track. Check it out here!
  • Research undertaken at the Center for System Design at the National Institute of Technology Karnataka, jointly advised by Professor Gangadharan KV and Professor Pruthviraj Umesh.
Gesture Controlled Robotic Arm
  • Built and designed a 3D printed robotic arm, controlled by human gestures through a Microsoft Kinect.
  • Open Natural Interactions Library for Processing was used to detect user's pose and joint angles.
  • Detected angles were translated to joint movements of the Arm by an Arduino Uno.
Fracture-Detection System
  • Built a fracture detection system to identify fractures and fatiguing in manufactured components, and predictions were made about the same.
  • The system employed OpenCV through which fractures were identified, after being subjected to different filters.
  • The TensorFlow model, based on ImageNet, retrained on a dataset of normal and fractured gear images, was used as an example to make predictions about wearability and test the system.
Ultrasonic Obstacle Avoider
  • Built an Arduino based mobile robot, capable of avoiding obstacles.
  • Bot comprised of three ultrasonic sensors oriented in three different directions which actively detect obstacles around them.
  • The project was a great starting point to learn the basics of Arduino programming.
Line Follower Bot
  • Built a line follower bot that followed a black stripe overlaid on a white track.
  • The Bot has an Arduino Uno onboard, which co-ordinates the movements of the bot with the sensor readings, making it follow a black line using three infrared sensors.
  • One of the first robots built once inducted into the robotics club.
